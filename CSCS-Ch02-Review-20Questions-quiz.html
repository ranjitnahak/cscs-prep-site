Ranjit Nahak, MSc, CSCS
Chapter 2 is the mechanical backbone of everything you'll programme as a strength coach. If levers and torque felt abstract, revisit the elbow and knee examples — they make the algebra concrete. For the force–velocity curve and impulse–momentum relationship, work through the numerical examples in the textbook until the equations feel intuitive, not just memorised. The joint biomechanics section is high-yield for the exam — back posture, IAP, and the difference between Valsalva and open-airway bracing come up regularly. Well done for completing the session check — use your domain scores to prioritise what to revisit next.
